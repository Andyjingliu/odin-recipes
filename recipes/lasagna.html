Lasagna

Medium Difficulty
Prep Time 30 mins
Cook Time 50 mins
Total Time 80 mins
Servings 8 servings

Description

This classic lasagna is a hearty Italian dish perfect for feeding a crowd or meal prepping for the week. It features layers of tender pasta sheets, a rich meat sauce made with ground beef and Italian sausage, and a creamy cheese filling. The combination of savory tomato sauce, melted mozzarella, and nutty Parmesan creates a comforting meal that tastes even better the next day.

This recipe balances the acidity of tomatoes with the richness of the meat and cheese. While it requires some assembly time, the result is a bubbling, golden-brown masterpiece that is well worth the effort. Serve it with a side of garlic bread and a fresh green salad for a complete dinner.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 lb Italian sausage, casings removed
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 28 oz can crushed tomatoes
  • 15 oz can tomato sauce
  • 6 oz tomato paste
  • 2 tsp dried basil
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 16 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh parsley, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions.
  3. In a large skillet, brown the ground beef and Italian sausage. Add onions and garlic, cook until softened.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato sauce, tomato paste, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. Simmer for 15 minutes.
  5. In a bowl, mix ricotta cheese with egg and half of the mozzarella cheese.
  6. Layer noodles, meat sauce, and cheese mixture in a baking dish. Repeat layers.
  7. Sprinkle remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheeses on top.
  8. Bake covered for 25 minutes. Uncover and bake for another 25 minutes until golden brown.
  9. Let rest for 10 minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh parsley if desired.

üí° Tips & Notes

  • Make Ahead: This dish can be assembled completely 1 day ahead. Cover with plastic wrap and refrigerate. Bake directly from the fridge, adding 10-15 minutes to the cooking time.
  • Freezer Friendly: Assemble the lasagna in a freezer-safe dish, wrap tightly, and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw overnight before baking.
  • No-Boil Noodles: You can use no-boil lasagna noodles for even easier preparation. Add an extra ¬Ω cup of water to the sauce.
  • Meat-Free Option: Replace the beef and sausage with 2 lbs of sliced mushrooms for a vegetarian version.
  • Resting Time: Don't skip the 10-minute rest! This allows the lasagna to set and makes slicing much cleaner.
  • Layering Tip: Start and end with sauce to prevent the pasta from drying out.
